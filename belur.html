Shri Chennakeshava Swami Temple Beluru





History

The Hoysala period of South Indian history began about 1000 CE and continued through 1346 CE. In this period, they built around 1,500 temples in 958 centres. Belur is called Beluhur, Velur or Velapura in old inscriptions and medieval era texts. It was the early capital of the Hoysala kings. The city was so esteemed by the Hoysalas that it is referred to as "earthly Vaikuntha" (Vishnu's abode). One of the Hoyasala kings was Vishnuvardhana, who came to power in 1110 CE. He commissioned the Chennakeshava temple dedicated to Vishnu in 1117 CE, a temple considered as one of "five foundations" of his legacy.[8][10] According to Dhaky â€“ a scholar of Indian temple architecture and temple history, this temple reflects the rising opulence, political power, deep spiritual dedication to Sri Vaishnavism of Ramanujacharya, and it is his magnum opus. The main temple is called Vijaya-Narayana and the smaller temple next to it built by his queen Santala Devi is called Chennakesava in the inscriptions of his era, but these two temples are now called the Chennakesava temple and Chennigaraya temple respectively.

Shri Kshethra Pushpagiri





history

Sri Pushpagiri Mahasamstana Math is one of the Religious institutions being run by the young, dynamic and scholarly Veerashaiva Pontiff Sri Sri Sri Somashekara Shivacharya Mahaswamiji. This Math has a very ancient and long history of over one thousand years. The Math is involved in multi-dimensional activities to serve the people of this region in general and specifically the local poverty-ridden poor people irrespective of caste, creed, gender, culture, or tradition. Thus Math is very well known for its Secularistic approach and service. The location of the Math is on a very splendid hillock called the Pushpagiri Hills. The history of these hills goes back 900 years to the regime of the then Hoysala Kingdom ruling the Southern Part of Karnataka. This Kingdom was responsible for the prosperous and glorious development of Karnataka. The Hills is the abode of Lord Mallikarjuna (Lord Shiva) and is the reigning deity of Pushpagiri. The temple was built by Queen Shanthala Devi, a renowned danseuse and the Queen of King Vishnuvarrdhana during the 11th Century. The Math has seen several pontiffs and it has a large group of followers around the globe. It has 4 branches in Hassan District which are serving society for the past 600 years. The present seer of the math Sri Sri Sri Siomashekhara Mahaswamiji is very young, dynamic and much interested in establishing and carrying out multifaceted activities in the math. In this regard, he is embarking on a very big project of Establishing a Local Museum for the benefit of society at large.

Ancient Hoysala Shri Doddagaddavalli Lakshmi Devi Temple





history

The temples complex is within a seven feet high prakara (compound), almost a 115 feet square. It had a dvara-mandapa to its west and a dvarasobha to its east, but these along with the original compound decoration is now missing except for the door frames and the mandapa-like structure.[1] It is likely that the original town was near the manmade reservoir. Inside the historic prakara were four small shrines at each corner, a design called parivaralayas in Sanskrit texts. In addition, to the northeast of the main temple, there is a fifth independent shrine dedicated to Bhairava. At the center is the chatuskuta (four-shrine) main temple with a shared mandapa. Thus, the complex consists of nine shrines.[1] The Lakshmi Devi Temple is one of the earliest known temples built in the Hoysala style. The building material is Chloritic schist, more commonly known as soapstone.[2]
